# Design: Influencers Nepal Business Model Analysis

Generated by /office-hours on 2026-03-31
Status: DRAFT
Mode: Startup

## Problem Statement
Nepal's creators struggle with self-promotion while local businesses (cafes, cleaning services) lack a structured way to find niche influencers who genuinely align with their service.

## Demand Evidence
- 70 creators and 4 businesses signed up for a demo site without marketing.
- Real requests from cafe/restaurant and cleaning service owners wanting creators to visit/test their services.

## Status Quo
Creators with huge reach get most opportunities; those with high social capital but 'smaller' reach are currently unable to monetize effectively.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge
- **Target Business:** Proprietorships like cleaning services and local cafes/restaurants.
- **Target Creator:** Niche experts (food bloggers, hygiene/home-care enthusiasts) with high social capital.
- **Wedge:** A 'visit-and-post' matchmaking service for local service-based businesses.

## Premises
1. The platform's core value is being a **Matchmaking Bridge** between two fragmented groups.

## Recommended Approach: Full Marketplace Integration
- **Automated Matching:** Algorithm to pair cleaning services with hygiene-focused creators.
- **Verified Profiles:** Ensure creators have real social capital.
- **Performance Tracking:** Basic analytics for businesses to see reach/engagement.
- **Chat & Collaborate:** Built-in tool for discussing campaign details.

## The Assignment
Go back to the 4 businesses that signed up. Ask them: 'If I could guarantee a visit from one food blogger this week for 2000 NPR, would you pay for it right now?' Verify the price point and willingness to pay immediately.
